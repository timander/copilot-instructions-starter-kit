# Acknowledgements

This kit's `expert-panel` skill names ten authors — Martin Fowler, Kent Beck, Joshua Kerievsky, Joshua Bloch, Robert C. Martin, J.B. Rainsberger, Sandi Metz, Michael Feathers, Ward Cunningham, and Kevlin Henney — and asks an AI to simulate their perspectives on a design question.

A few things I want to be clear about.

## What this is

The skill draws on **publicly published work**: books, talks, essays, conference recordings. Any "voice" the AI uses is inferred from that public material — nothing else. There is no private correspondence, no claim of endorsement, no representation that anything the AI says is what these authors would actually say. The panel is a study tool: a way to stress-test a design decision against thinking these authors have already put into the world.

## Why I'm naming them

Each one has had a profound impact on my thinking and my career. Reading *Refactoring*, *Effective Java*, *POODR*, *Working Effectively with Legacy Code*, *Clean Code*, *Tidy First?*, *Refactoring to Patterns* — and following their talks, essays, and conference sessions over many years — shaped how I write code, how I review it, and how I share it with engineers earlier in their careers. It is an honor to convene them, even in this small simulated form, around the problems I work on every day. Thank you for publishing what you've published.

## If you're one of them

If you're one of the authors named above and you'd prefer not to be included, or you'd like the framing changed, or a citation corrected — please [open an issue](https://github.com/timander/copilot-instructions-starter-kit/issues) or [reach out via my contact page](https://timandersen.net/contact/). I'll respond promptly. The intent here is gratitude and study, not imitation.
